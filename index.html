Over 9 years in fintech, insurance, and enterprise platforms, I’ve built a career in the environments most PMs avoid — heavily regulated, multi-stakeholder, structurally broken. The kind where the real problem is never the stated problem.

A few examples: At Ping An, I turned a market the industry had written off as uninsurable into a $2M product — not by taking on more risk, but by redesigning the incentive structure underneath it. At Accenture, I broke a three-continent stakeholder deadlock by stopping trying to persuade anyone and instead redesigning the rules — a tiered rollout architecture that let both sides win, later adopted as Discovery’s global emerging market standard. And when I spotted an untouched gap in a client’s claims workflow, I built the ROI case myself, assembled the team, and shipped an NLP solution in 8 weeks with $420K in projected annual savings — with zero mandate at the start.

What I bring that’s harder to find: business model intuition built on unit economics and risk modeling; technical depth in SQL, Python, and ML implementation that I use to find leverage, not just describe requirements; and a consistent track record of identifying opportunities no one asked me to find — then driving them to results.

If the problem looks impossible, that’s usually where I start.
